## Letter Ε (Epsilon): On the Infinitesimal and Precision
## Ε 之信：關於無窮小與精確

Dear Reader,

Epsilon is the mathematician's way of saying "arbitrarily small but not zero." In epsilon-delta proofs, we show that we can make outputs as close as we want to targets by making inputs sufficiently close. Epsilon captures the essence of limits, continuity, and convergence.

In programming, epsilon represents floating-point precision limits - the smallest difference our computers can reliably distinguish. Testing equality of floats requires epsilon tolerance: not x == y, but |x - y| < ε. Ignoring epsilon leads to subtle bugs, infinite loops, and computational chaos.

The epsilon-greedy strategy in reinforcement learning balances exploitation and exploration: with probability ε, try something new; otherwise, use what works. Small ε means conservative learning; large ε means aggressive exploration. Even small changes in epsilon dramatically affect outcomes.

Wave-particle duality means quantum objects behave like waves (described by ψ) or particles (described by measurements) depending on context. This isn't two aspects of reality; it's one reality transcending classical categories. Psi represents this fundamental strangeness at the heart of physics.
